How it works

Calendar to timesheet in 3 steps

No manual time entry. No separate app. Your calendar events are already your time log — we just format them for billing.

1

Color your events

Use Google Calendar™ event colors to mark projects — Tomato for Client A, Basil for Client B. You probably do this already.

2

Map colors to projects

Open the sidebar, assign each color to a project name and hourly rate. Set keyword rules for events you don't color. Save once, use forever.

3

Generate your timesheet

Pick a date range, click Generate. Get a formatted timesheet with hours, billing amounts, project summaries, and a review tab catching billing errors.

Features

Built for freelancers who bill hourly

Everything you need to go from calendar to invoice — nothing you don't.

Zero-effort categorization

Map event colors to projects and hourly rates. Tomato = Client A at $150/hr. No new workflow to learn — you already color your events.

Smart date presets

This Week, Last Week, This Month, Last Month, or custom range. Billing cycles are built into the workflow.

3 tabs, 1 click

Generates a Timesheet tab (every event), Summary tab (totals by project), and Review tab (flagged issues) — all formatted and ready.

Billing error detection

Catches uncategorized events, overlapping meetings, untitled entries, and suspiciously short events. Your compliance officer in a tab.

Proper timezone handling

All times display in your local timezone. No UTC, no Zulu time, no offset confusion. Handles DST transitions automatically.

Recurring event expansion

Weekly standups, daily check-ins — every recurring instance becomes its own row. No missed hours, no manual splitting.

Keyword matching

Don't color every event? Set rules: title contains "Acme" → Client A. Color mapping and keyword rules work together.

One-click refresh

After first setup, regenerate your timesheet from the menu without reopening the sidebar. Extensions → Refresh Timesheet → done.
